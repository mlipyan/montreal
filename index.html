Along the storied banks of the St. Lawrence — towns, forests, and the sweet smoke of sugar shacks

🗺 The Road — Chemin du Roy ▼
  • ⸻
    Chemin du Roy Historic road along the north shore of the St. Lawrence. Begins in Repentigny, extends ~280 km east to Québec City. Follows present-day Route 138.

🏰 Sites & Activities ▼
  • ◆
    Old QuébecEntirely walkable — start here and let the cobblestones lead you
  • ◆
    Walk the City WallsThe only remaining fortified city walls in North America north of Mexico — a 4.6 km loop with remarkable views
  • ◆
    Fairmont Le Château FrontenacThe most photographed hotel in the world — stop in for a drink, or simply stand outside and look up
  • ◆
    Citadelle of QuébecA star-shaped fortress built in the 1820s, still garrisoned by the Royal 22e Régiment — catch the summer cannon firing
  • ◆
    Morrin CentreA 19th-century prison converted into a literary and cultural hub, housing one of Québec's oldest English libraries
  • ◆
    House of LiteratureQuébec's literary museum in a converted church — the architecture alone is worth the visit
  • ◆
    Lampadaires — Avenue CartierThe neighbourhood's beloved lampshade installation casts a warm, playful glow over the café terrasses
  • ◆
    Papeterie Saint-GillesA working paper mill in nearby Saint-Joseph-de-la-Rive — watch artisans at work and make your own sheet of handmade paper
  • ◆
    Isle aux CoudresA quiet island in the St. Lawrence with cycling paths, windmills, and a legendary schooner museum
  • ◆
    Strom Spa Nordique 🧖Outdoor Nordic spa with thermotherapy baths overlooking the St. Lawrence
  • ◆
    Siberian Station SpaA beautifully designed urban Nordic spa in the heart of the city
  • ◆
    Nordik Spa Nature VillageA sprawling Nordic wellness retreat in the forest — thermotherapy pools, wood-fired saunas, and genuine quiet
🌳 Nature & Views ▼
  • ◆
    Île d'OrléansA pastoral island adrift in the St. Lawrence — farm stands, chocolatiers, cideries, and some of the province's best sugar shack views
  • ◆
    Terrasse du Chevalier-de-LévisThe viewpoint locals swear by — Château Frontenac and the old city skyline at golden hour, seen from Lévis across the water
  • ◆
    Montmorency FallsTaller than Niagara — a thundering waterfall just 10 minutes from the old city, with a suspension bridge at the crest
  • ◆
    Cap-Tourmente 🌳A national wildlife area on the north shore — extraordinary during the spring and fall snow goose migrations
